sTELgano is an anonymous messaging protocol where conversations hide behind phone numbers saved in your contacts. No accounts. No history. No trace.

End-to-end encrypted

AES-256-GCM encryption happens entirely in your browser. The server never sees your messages.

No accounts

No email, no phone verification, no profile. Just a shared number and a PIN you both choose.

N=1 messaging

Only one message exists at a time. Replies atomically delete the previous message. No history anywhere.

Security

How sTELgano protects your conversations.

Threat Model

sTELgano protects against intimate-access attackers: a partner, family member, or anyone with physical access to your unlocked device. It does not protect against governments or law enforcement with legal authority to compel data.

We are honest about this limitation because false promises of absolute security can put vulnerable people in danger.

Client-Side Encryption

All cryptographic operations happen in your browser using the Web Crypto API. The server only ever sees SHA-256 hashes (for room identification) and AES-256-GCM ciphertext (your encrypted messages).

Encryption keys are derived using PBKDF2 with 600,000 iterations, meeting the OWASP 2023 recommendation for password-based key derivation.

Zero Knowledge

The server cannot read your messages, identify your phone number, or determine your PIN. Room access is verified using hashed credentials only.
