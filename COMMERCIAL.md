# Commercial Licensing

sTELgano is released under the GNU Affero General Public License v3 (AGPL-3.0).
The AGPL is a strong copyleft licence — if you run a modified version of sTELgano
as a network service, you must publish your modifications under the same terms.

## When you need a commercial licence

You need a commercial licence if:

- You want to run sTELgano as a service **without** publishing your modifications
- You want to embed sTELgano's protocol or cryptographic implementation into a
  proprietary product
- You want a written warranty, indemnification, or SLA beyond what the AGPL provides
- Your legal or compliance team requires a proprietary licence for internal use

## What commercial licences cover

| Tier | For | Includes |
|------|-----|----------|
| **Organisation** | Teams, companies, NGOs, legal firms | Private deployment, no source-disclosure obligation, 12-month support |
| **OEM / Integration** | Embedding the protocol in another product | Protocol licence, written integration agreement |
| **Managed Hosting** | You want us to run it for you | Hosted deployment on your domain, SLA, backups, updates |

## What commercial licensing does NOT change

The AGPL version remains freely available forever. We will never:
- Remove the public AGPL release
- Retroactively relicence existing open-source versions
- Restrict self-hosting for personal or non-commercial use

Commercial licensing funds the ongoing development and maintenance of the
open-source project. It is how we stay solvent without compromising the product.
