Why sTELgano

Privacy that doesn't advertise itself.

Most privacy apps scream “I have something to hide.” sTELgano takes a different approach—starting with the name itself, which is designed to mean nothing to a casual observer. Your encrypted channel lives inside a phone number saved in your contacts—invisible to anyone who picks up your phone.

Zero-Knowledge Architecture.

The server is entirely blind. It acts only as a dumb relay for hashes and ciphertext, knowing absolutely nothing about you or your data.

  • Opaque by Default

    No plaintext ever touches the backend. Even a full database breach reveals nothing but uncrackable hashes and AES-256-GCM ciphertext.

  • Untrusted Backend

    The protocol is designed so you don't even have to trust us. The cryptography guarantees your privacy strictly on the client side.

Ephemeral Identity.

No accounts. No signups. No traces. Your identity is generated strictly on-the-fly and vanishes the moment you close the tab.

  • No Email, No Phone

    We don't want to know who you are. The system is designed to prevent us from ever tying a session to a real-world identity.

  • Memory-Only Sessions

    Session keys are kept strictly in volatile memory. Close the browser, and the encryption keys are wiped from existence forever.

Client-Side Cryptography.

The keys never leave your device. All encryption and decryption happens strictly inside your local browser via the Web Crypto API.

  • Local Key Derivation

    Your encryption keys are derived from your PIN and the Steg Number using 600,000 rounds of PBKDF2 directly on your CPU.

  • End-to-End by Design

    Because the payload is encrypted with AES-256-GCM before it ever hits the network, interception is mathematically impossible to decode.

The Core Invariant

N=1 Messaging.

sTELgano enforces a strict N=1 invariant at the database level. At most, one message can exist per room at any given time.

  • Instant Annihilation

    The moment a reply is sent, the previous message is atomically wiped from the database in a single transaction.

  • No Chat History

    Because old messages are actively destroyed to make room for new ones, there is no scrollback. No logs. No history to recover.

  • Zero Long-Term Liability

    Even if a server is physically seized or fully compromised, there is nothing to extract except the single current ciphertext.

Security Model

Transparent about what we store.

We believe you should know exactly what data touches our servers. Here's the full picture—no fine print, no exceptions.

What the server stores

  • Room hash— SHA-256 of the steg number. Cannot be reversed to the original number.
  • Access hash— SHA-256 of number + PIN. Proves access rights without revealing the PIN.
  • Encrypted messages— AES-256-GCM ciphertext. Unreadable without the client-side key.
  • TTL expiry timer— Optional room self-destruct timestamp. Nothing else.

What the server never sees

  • Phone numbers— Only hashes. The server cannot determine the original steg number.
  • PINs— Hashed client-side before transmission. Never stored or logged in any form.
  • Plaintext messages— Encrypted in your browser before sending. Decrypted only on the recipient's device.
  • Encryption keys— Derived via PBKDF2 in your browser. Never transmitted. Never stored.
  • IP addresses— Stripped from logs within 48 hours. Never correlated with rooms.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions.

How does contact-layer steganography work?

Unlike other apps where you have an obvious icon on your home screen, sTELgano hides in plain sight. You generate a random “Steg Number” and save it in your phone's address book under any name you choose. To an observer, it's just a normal contact. But when you enter that number into sTELgano, it derives the encryption keys and opens your secure channel.

What happens to my messages?

sTELgano strictly enforces an N=1 invariant. This means only one message can exist per room at any given time. The moment a reply is sent, the previous message is atomically wiped from the database. There is no chat history to delete, because history is never retained.

Can the server read my messages?

No. All encryption happens client-side in your browser using the Web Crypto API. The payload is encrypted with AES-256-GCM before it ever leaves your device. The server only sees ciphertext and cannot decipher it because it never receives your keys or PIN.

If there's no account, how do I log in?

Your identity is ephemeral. You access a room by knowing the shared Steg Number and your private PIN. When you enter them, your browser mathematically derives the access hashes required to connect. No email, no password, no 2FA.

What happens if I forget my PIN?

You lose access to the room forever. Because sTELgano is a zero-knowledge system, we don't know your PIN and have no way to reset it. You will need to coordinate with your contact to generate a new Steg Number and start a new room.
